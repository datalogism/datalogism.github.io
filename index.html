In brief,

From numbers to texts, I obtained a Datascience master degree and I am playing with language since 2016. I spend three years with DataObserver developing information monitoring solutions. I worked on NLP, dataviz, and graph questions for managing a big stream of multimodal news and scientific content for controversy analysis. Interested in social and humanist questions, I complete a Digital Humanities master in 2018 at the University Lyon II. I discovered web semantic by working on culturally linked open data in 2020 at the Ottawa DH Lab. I became research engineer in the Wimmics team of Inria Sophia-Antipolis. I had the opportunity to update and extend the french chapter of DBpedia for a year, which I am still maintaining. For the moment, i'm doing a Phd Thesis thanks the 3IA convention.

ISSA Hackaton

With Benjamin Molinet we participate in the ISSA Hackathon for two days from the 28th to the 29th of October. The ISSA project is a scientific open data hub focused on biological and agriculture research. During this two days coding marathon, we developed a framework for publication analysis based SPARQL query specificity index computation, and language model-based keywords retrieval.

GSOC project - DBpedia Abstract

From June to September 2022 I participate to the Goggle Summer Of Code. This event allow development of open source projects via a monitored project that each project team submitted. DBpedia was one of the association that monitored projects : DBpedia provides monthly releases produced by the DBpedia Extraction Framework. They are composed of various data artifacts that mainly stem from the wiki dumps. However, some of them also rely on API calls for rendering dynamic contents, which is the case of the DBpedia abstracts. The large amount of data requested from APIs couldn't be extracted entirely within a month today. For solving it i tried severals stragtegies : a wikimedia docker clone, I also corrected current API usage and extended the DBpedia code source for being able to call an alternative API, that could be use for getting the entire text of a wikipedia page.

Harry Potter Mining

In 2021, I tried to obtain a CAPES NSI, a new french diploma for teaching algorithm, and IT base in high schools. Of 1000 candidates only 100 places were given. Without enough preparation, I failed it. The exam includes teaching assessments based on the program. I choose to introduce data mining technics, based on the Harry Potter books analysis. From the extraction of the chapter, the main characters, and the emotions, we can explore the evolution of the social network of Hogwarts. We also introduce classification algorithms on which the Sorting Hat evaluates if a new scholar must the Slytherins.
